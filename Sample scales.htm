Sample scales for Pawn — Turtle WoW 1.18

These scales are designed for Turtle WoW (patch 1.18, vanilla 1.12 client). They cover all nine base classes with one scale per common spec. Weights are derived from community sources including the VanillaRatingBuster addon and Turtle WoW forum guides.

Important: These are starting points, not gospel. Vanilla stat weights shift with gear level and raid tier. Stat values here use vanilla’s direct percentage stats (Hit %, Crit %) rather than rating-based systems from later expansions. Adjust values to match your gear and preferences.

How to use Pawn scale tags

Each scale includes a Pawn scale tag that you can use to automatically add the scale to Pawn for your character.  To use a scale tag:

  1. Highlight all of the scale tag, from the "(" to the ")", and then press Ctrl+C to copy it.
  2. In World of Warcraft, type /pawn in the chat window to open the Pawn configuration UI.
  3. Click the Import button.
  4. A window will appear.  Press Ctrl+V to paste the scale tag into it.
  5. Click OK.
  6. Hover over an item or click an item link to see the scale in action!

Community resources

Further discussion and tuned stat weights can be found on the Turtle WoW community forums. The VanillaRatingBuster addon provides in-tooltip ratings for several specs and is the primary source for the feral druid, warrior, paladin, priest, and shaman weights below.

Warrior

Warriors have three main specs in Turtle WoW: Arms (two-handed PvE/PvP), Fury (dual-wield DPS), and Protection (tank). Physical Hit % is the top priority for DPS specs until the cap (9 % for special abilities with Weapon Mastery talent, 14 % for white swings with Fury). 1 Strength = 2 Attack Power for warriors.

Hunter

Hunters deal primarily Ranged Attack Power-based damage. Agility provides 2 Ranged AP per point and also increases crit chance. Intellect extends mana for sustained DPS in long fights. All three specs (BM, MM, Survival) use largely the same gear priorities.

Rogue

Rogues are highly dependent on weapon DPS and physical Hit. Agility gives 2 AP per point and also crit chance. Combat spec favors a fast main-hand and a high-DPS off-hand; Subtlety and Assassination favor the main-hand dagger above all else.
